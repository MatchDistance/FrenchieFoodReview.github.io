Raspberry Croissant
Taste: 9/10 Frenchuraccy: 10/10
Really good looking pastry, with a little bit of white sugar on top. It looks and feels like a french croissant texture wise. The taste on the other hand is nothing like a traditional french croissant (or ‘paint au chocolat’ more accurately). It has a ‘mille feuilles’ side of it with its multiple layers and soft dough near the center. Its taste was very pleasant. The pastry didn’t feel very sugary but still had a pronounced fruity flavor that goes really well with the bread.
Mini Chocolate Tart
Taste: 4/10 Frenchuraccy: 9/10
Minimalistic one bite chocolate tart with whip cream on top. The biscuit has no flavor but has a nice crispy feel. The chocolate cream on top is strong compared to the flavors of the 2 other cream making it a disappointing experience. The mini tart is definitely something you would find in any pastry shop in france especially with its simple presentation and it’s tiny chocolate curl.
French Dip

Buche de Noel
Taste: 9/10 Frenchuraccy: 10/10
Very light famous christmas french pastry. It’s the regular version of the buche de noel and is very delicious. Didn’t give a 10 for the taste because I didn't feel like it was much different than most american cakes with the cream and the soft paste.
